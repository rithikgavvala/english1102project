Controlled of the Uncontrolled

Critical introduction

However, a user may not have as much control over a story as they initially believe. If a choice is given over two parallel paths a number of times, an exponential number of endings and possibilities is formed. A human programmer, however, will never be able to represent all of these possibilities. Thus, the programmer, must limit the outcomes in order for the piece to end, and when users fiddle with electronic work long enough, this restriction on the number of attainable story routes becomes evident.

For instance, in the story Inanimate Alice, everything leading up to the climax involves the reader clicking through to advance the story, but the climax itself has no interaction: the story just plays without any user input. This lack of control the reader feels is mirrored perfectly by the lack of control felt by the protagonist as she searches for her lost father. This isn't just some metaphor lost on the casual reader, it's an experience shared by all readers along with Alice herself.
