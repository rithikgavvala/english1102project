Controlled of the Uncontrolled

Critical introduction

Text entry as well as the incorporation of video and buttons allow the user to feel even more like they are a part of the story, therefore increasing engagement. In “Black Mirror: Bandersnatch”, the viewer controls the main character Stefan through choosing different options for him. Whenever the user makes a choice, the result can quickly be seen taking effect on Stefan. While the visuals certainly help increase engagement over traditional print literature because humans are naturally wired to respond empathetically to pain, happiness, et cetera, the greatest emotional engagement comes from the fact that the user feels responsible for creating whichever emotion is seen. Therefore, whenever Stefan fails or the movie is reset to a previous decision the user is left with the nagging curiosity of why, which choice caused this. This forces the user to think about the plot and what they could do differently. Subsequently, a user will try different options and engage with a piece far longer than they would with traditional literature because they feel a sense of control and don’t want to fail.
