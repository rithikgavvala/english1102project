Controlled of the Uncontrolled

Critical introduction

Interactivity within a piece harnesses two innate human characteristics in order to enhance engagement: curiosity, and short attention spans. By nature, humans are constantly curious and do not actually need separate endings in order to be left wondering what if, what would have happened had they chosen a different option somewhere along the line. By keeping the reader constantly wondering about these supposedly different paths they could have taken, authors of electronic literature capitalize upon short attention spans. Especially in this day and age readers are constantly assaulted by different notifications and pop ups vying for their attention. The different onscreen actions a user must take in order to progress the story forward reel readers back in by making sure they are actively engaging with and thinking about the piece.
